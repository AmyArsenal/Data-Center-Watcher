About

What we do, and why.

Data Center Watcher is a real-time tracker of US data-center opposition — bills, news, organized community resistance, court rulings, and prediction-market signals — built for developers, investors, utilities, and policy teams that need to know what's happening before it shows up in trade press.

Mission

Roughly half of US data-center capacity planned for 2026 is delayed or cancelled. Reuters, NPR, and Bloomberg have all covered the top-line story. What's missing is the per-state, per-county, weekly-resolution view of where the resistance is moving and what comes next.

We exist to give that view to anyone whose job depends on understanding it — and to make the underlying data accessible enough that journalists, academics, and activists can use it too.

How it works

Three independent pipelines run continuously:

Fast tier — every 15 minutes, GitHub Actions pulls from GDELT (global news), Reddit JSON (community discussions), eight outlet RSS feeds (Data Center Dynamics, Utility Dive, Ars Technica, RTO Insider, etc.), and CourtListener (legal filings). Results are cross-source dedup'd, classified by state and category, and published to data/news.json.

Bills tier — every hour, GitHub Actions queries OpenStates' v3 REST API across all 50 states for new DC-related legislation. Each bill is classified into one of four tiers (restrictive, protective, supportive, unclear) using a rule-based keyword classifier. Published to data/bills.json.

Deep tier — once daily, a laptop-local script runs the open-source last30days skill against social platforms (X, YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, Threads, Polymarket, HackerNews) and ingests the results into the same database. Published to data/social_events.json.

All three pipelines feed a single SQLite store with cross-source deduplication via URL canonicalization plus content hashing — so when 12 outlets cover the same Maine moratorium, it appears as one event with a list of 12 sources, not 12 duplicate cards.

Source mix is by design. News alone is a lagging indicator — reporters write after the council vote. Reddit + Polymarket are leading. Bills are forward-looking. Cross-source dedup means we keep the strongest signal of each story without duplication.

What we don't do

We don't take political sides. Hyperscalers, utilities, activist coalitions, hedge funds with both long and short DC exposure are all welcome subscribers. Our job is to surface the signal accurately. Yours is to act on it.

We don't sell user-organizing data to corporate clients, and we don't sell corporate-strategy data to organizers. The dashboard is the same dashboard for everyone.

We don't publish AI-generated copy without a human edit. The weekly brief is drafted with help from Claude Haiku for entity extraction and synthesis, then a human editor sets the framing, verifies citations, and signs every issue.

Open source

The scrapers, classifiers, and the dashboard frontend are all public on GitHub. We borrow from OpenStates (bill data), GDELT (news), and the open-source last30days skill (social aggregation) — all properly attributed, all citing back to the originating source on every event.
