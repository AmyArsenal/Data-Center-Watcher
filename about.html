About

The power side is covered. The social side isn't.

Substations, PPAs, interconnection queues. The sell side has tracked all of it for two decades. What no one tracks: the city councils, the county boards, the recall petitions, the ballot drives. That's the half killing projects in 2026, and that's what we built.

What we built

Roughly half the US data center capacity planned for 2026 is delayed or cancelled. Reuters and Bloomberg have the headline. Nobody has the per-county, per-week, per-bill view that actually tells you which sites survive.

Every county vote. Every state bill. Every recall, ballot drive, and lawsuit. Refreshed every fifteen minutes. Mapped, ranked, searchable.

Why it matters

A single missed county moratorium can void $50M of land options and 18 months of permitting work. The signal exists weeks before it reaches the trade press. Until now, the only way to catch it was to staff a research analyst per state.

We do that work. You read the dashboard for free. Pro subscribers get the weekly brief, county dossiers, and the data behind the map.

Who reads us

Site selection teams, utility planners, REIT and hyperscaler analysts, statehouse policy and legal teams, journalists, and the community organizers running the votes themselves. We treat them all the same. Same dashboard, same data, same price.

What we don't do

We don't take political sides. Hyperscalers, utilities, activist coalitions, hedge funds long and short on data center exposure all subscribe. Our job is to surface the signal. Yours is to act on it.

We don't sell organizing data to corporate clients or corporate-strategy data to organizers. The dashboard is the same for everyone.

We don't publish AI-written copy. Everything you read here was written by a human, with citations.
