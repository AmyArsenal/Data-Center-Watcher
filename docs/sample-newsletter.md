## 🔥 THE BIG STORY — Maine LD 307 is no longer theoretical

Maine's 20 MW moratorium through November 2027 is now the first statewide precedent in US history. Opposition researchers on Reddit have spent this week pulling it apart:

- **"Maine's 20 MW Line Shows the Real Bottleneck Is Power, Not Chips"** — the cleanest one-line framing we've seen. 20 MW sits *below* hyperscaler economics, meaning Maine has effectively banned anything a Microsoft or Meta would build, while leaving sub-scale and co-located options untouched. That's not an accident.
- **"Maine just made microgrids part of the data center approval process."** LD 307 quietly requires siting review to consider microgrid/behind-the-meter alternatives. This is the policy language that will get copy-pasted in other states.
- **"The 20 MW pause in Maine points to a much bigger AI constraint."** [[r/energy](#)]

**What to watch:** ten different outlets and Reddit threads converged on Maine this week. Nine months ago this would have been a local-outlet story. That's the signal.
